Odisha - Scenic, Serene, Sublime!


Odisha is famous for its classical dance forms, particularly Odissi, one of the oldest dance forms in India. The state is home to numerous temples showcasing exquisite Kalinga architecture, with the Sun Temple at Konark and the Jagannath Temple in Puri being the most notable.The state is known for its vibrant festivals, including the Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) in Puri, which attracts thousands of devotees. Other significant festivals include Durga Puja, Diwali, and Makar Mela. Odisha features stunning landscapes, from the serene beaches of Puri and Gopalpur to the breathtaking hills of the Eastern Ghats. The Chilika Lake, Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, is a hotspot for migratory birds and offers a unique ecosystem.


5 Must-See Gems of Odisha:


  • Puri:

    Famous for its stunning beaches and the iconic Jagannath Temple, Puri is a spiritual hub and a vibrant destination for tourists. Don’t miss the annual Rath Yatra festival!


  • Konark:

    Home to the breathtaking Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Konark is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings. The temple is designed in the shape of a colossal chariot.


  • Bhubaneswar:

    The capital city, often referred to as the "Temple City of India," is rich in history and culture. Explore ancient temples like Lingaraj and Mukteshwar, showcasing exquisite Kalinga architecture.


  • Bhitarkanika National Park:

    A unique ecosystem teeming with wildlife, this national park is famous for its mangroves, saltwater crocodiles, and diverse bird species. It’s an excellent spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers.


  • Chilika Lake:

    Asia’s largest brackish water lagoon, Chilika Lake is a paradise for bird watchers and nature enthusiasts. Enjoy boat rides, watch migratory birds, and savor fresh seafood at the lakeside villages.

5 Must-Try Cuisines of Odisha:


  • Pakhala Bhata:

    A staple in most Odia households, Pakhala Bhata is a fermented rice dish that is refreshing and cooling, perfect for summer. It’s often served with fried vegetables, fish, and papad.


  • Chenna Poda:

    A signature dessert of Odisha, Chenna Poda is made from fresh cottage cheese and baked to perfection. Its caramelized top gives it a unique flavor.


  • Khaja:

    Khaja recipe is a traditional coastal recipe that is served as a part of “Sukhila Prasad” in India’s Puri Jagannath temple. This is a sweet dish recipe, prepared from fewer ingredients, is essentially the layered fritters dunked in sugar syrup.Enjoy Khaja, one of the “Chappan Bhoga” (56 types of food served as Prasad at Jagannath Temple, Puri) to savor its festive taste during tea time or as a dessert option to end a festive meal.


  • Dahibara Aloodum:

    A popular street food, this dish combines soft vadas soaked in curd (Dahibara) with spicy potato curry (Aloodum) and tangy tamarind chutney.


  • Dalma:

    This staple food comes with a unique twist when in Odisha. It is made with roasted moong dal without any onion or garlic. A few regular spices along with a cup of veggies are added to it to give it a lip-smacking flavour. Common veggies include pumpkin, plantain, yam and papaya, which work like magic on your overall health. Mostly eaten with rice, this finger-licking meal will more than satiate your appetite.
